Welcome

I am a Ph.D. Candidate and Graduate Research Assistant at the University of California, Merced, working in the Forest Ecohydrology & Watershed Systems (FEWS) Lab under the supervision of Professor Safeeq Khan and Professor Joshua Viers. I am a passionate hydrologist and environmental systems researcher committed to developing sustainable water management strategies for climate-stressed regions.

My research focuses on modeling surface water and groundwater interactions across California’s endorheic basins, with specific attention to the effects of groundwater pumping and land subsidence in the Central Valley. I integrate advanced tools—such as SWAT+, MODFLOW (ModelMuse & Groundwater Vistas), HYDRUS, and Analytical Depletion Functions (ADF)—to support forest restoration, water balance modeling, and policy-relevant decision-making for drought resilience and ecological restoration in the Western United States.
