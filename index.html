Welcome

I am a Ph.D. Candidate and Graduate Research Assistant at the University of California, Merced, working in the Forest Ecohydrology & Watershed Systems (FEWS) Lab under the supervision of Professor Safeeq Khan and Professor Joshua Viers.

As a passionate hydrologist and water resources researcher, my work is rooted in understanding, protecting, and sustainably managing water systems, with a particular focus on the Western United States.

My research explores the dynamics of surface water–groundwater interactions in endorheic basins under changing climatic conditions. I apply advanced hydrological modeling tools including SWAT+, HYDRUS, MODFLOW (via Groundwater Vistas and ModelMuse), and Analytical Depletion Functions (ADF) to analyze complex watershed processes and develop data-driven solutions for climate-resilient water management.
